MPU-6050 IMU

Measures angular velocity and linear acceleration to compute the robot's tilt angle with high precision — the foundation of the self-balancing system.

PID Control Loop

Continuously calculates the error between target angle and measured angle, then adjusts motor speed and direction to correct it in milliseconds.

ESP32 AP Mode

Creates its own WiFi Access Point — your phone connects directly without a router. Commands sent over WebSocket or UDP for ultra-low latency.

Stepper Accuracy

Unlike DC motors, stepper motors move in discrete steps — giving the PID controller precise, predictable wheel movement critical for balance on uneven surfaces.

Step by Step

How It Works

From sensor data to motor movement — the complete flow of Aero Bot's self-balancing system. Even kids can understand it!

1

Sense Tilt

The MPU-6050 reads gyroscope and accelerometer data to determine Aero Bot's current tilt angle in real time.

🤔 Think of it like your inner ear telling your brain when you're about to fall!
2

Compute Error

The ESP32 calculates the difference between the desired angle (upright) and the actual angle using the PID algorithm.

🧮 Like a calculator figuring out how far off you are from standing perfectly straight!
3

Drive Motors

Motor drivers receive corrected speed and direction signals, adjusting the wheels to counteract the tilt instantly.

🚗 Like pressing the gas or brake on a car to stop yourself from tipping over!
4

Receive Commands

Simultaneously, the ESP32 listens for WiFi commands from the app, blending user input with balance correction seamlessly.

📱 Like talking to Aero Bot through your phone — it listens while still staying balanced!
5

Move & Balance

Aero Bot moves in your commanded direction while continuously maintaining its upright balance — all in milliseconds.

🎉 And it all happens so fast you can't even see it — like magic, but it's science!
